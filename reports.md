# PromptLens Examples — Professional Reports & Documents

---

## Example 1 — Business Report

**❌ Before:**
```
write a report on our sales performance
```

**PromptLens Score:** 2/10

**✅ PromptLens Improved:**
```
Write a 500-word internal sales performance report for Q3 2024.

Context: Our SaaS product saw 12% revenue growth but customer churn increased from
4% to 7%. New customer acquisition was up 20% but existing customers are leaving faster.

Audience: Senior management team — they know the numbers, they want analysis and
recommendations, not a recap.

Structure:
- Executive summary (2 sentences)
- What went well (bullet points)
- Key concern: the churn problem (1 paragraph with possible causes)
- 3 recommended actions with brief rationale

Tone: Direct and professional. No corporate fluff.
Do not pad. If a point can be made in one sentence, use one sentence.
Do not use: "leverage", "synergy", "actionable insights", "moving forward".
```

## Example 2 — Email to a Client

**❌ Before:**
```
write an email to a client about a delayed project
```

**✅ PromptLens Improved:**
```
Write a professional email to a client informing them that their website project
will be delayed by 2 weeks due to unexpected technical issues with their requested
payment integration.

Tone: Apologetic but confident. Acknowledge the delay without over-apologising.
Offer a revised deadline and one goodwill gesture (e.g. a free extra revision round).

Keep it under 150 words.
Do not start with "I hope this email finds you well."
Do not use passive voice ("mistakes were made") — own the issue directly.
```

## Example 3 — Research Summary

**❌ Before:**
```
summarize the latest research on remote work productivity
```

**✅ PromptLens Improved:**
```
Summarize what is well-established in research about remote work and productivity
as of your knowledge cutoff.

Focus on:
- What the evidence says about productivity impact (positive and negative findings)
- Which types of workers or roles are most/least affected
- Key factors that determine whether remote work helps or hurts output

Format: 4 short paragraphs. No bullet points.
Audience: An HR manager making a policy decision.

Important: Do not invent studies or statistics. If you reference a finding,
indicate how well-established it is ("multiple studies suggest..." vs
"one small study found..."). If you're uncertain, say so.
```

## Example 4 — Job Application Cover Letter

**❌ Before:**
```
write me a cover letter for a marketing job
```

**✅ PromptLens Improved:**
```
Write a cover letter for a Senior Content Marketing Manager role at a B2B SaaS company.

About me:
- 5 years experience in content marketing at tech startups
- Grew organic blog traffic by 300% at my last company
- Led a team of 3 writers
- Strong in SEO, editorial strategy, and cross-functional collaboration

Tone: Confident and direct. Show personality — not a generic template.
Length: 3 paragraphs max.

Do NOT:
- Start with "I am writing to express my interest in..."
- Use "passionate", "dynamic", "results-driven", or "team player"
- Sound like every other cover letter

DO:
- Lead with the most impressive result (traffic growth)
- Connect my experience directly to what a B2B SaaS company needs
- End with a clear, confident call to action
```
