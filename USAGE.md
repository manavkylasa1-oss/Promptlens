# 🔍 PromptLens — How to Use It (Complete Guide)

> Get better answers from any AI — Claude, ChatGPT, DeepSeek, Gemini, and more.

This guide explains exactly how to use PromptLens on every major AI platform.
No coding required. No installation. Just copy, paste, and go.

---

## What is PromptLens?

PromptLens turns any AI into a **prompt coach**. Instead of getting a generic answer,
PromptLens first:

- Scores your prompt across 6 dimensions
- Shows you exactly what's weak and why
- Explains how the AI is reading your request
- Rewrites it into a stronger version
- Teaches you what changed so you improve next time

---

## Before You Start — Get the PromptLens Text

Every method below requires you to copy the PromptLens system prompt first.

### How to copy it:
1. Go to this GitHub repository
2. Click on the file called **`promptlens.md`**
3. Click the **"Raw"** button (top right of the file)
4. Press **Ctrl+A** (Windows/Linux) or **Cmd+A** (Mac) to select everything
5. Press **Ctrl+C** or **Cmd+C** to copy

You now have PromptLens copied and ready. Follow the guide for your AI below.

## 🤖 Claude (claude.ai)

### Method 1 — Quick Session Use (No account settings needed)
Best for: Trying it once or occasionally

1. Go to [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) and start a **New Chat**
2. Click in the message box at the bottom
3. **Paste** the entire PromptLens text you copied (`Ctrl+V` or `Cmd+V`)
4. Press **Enter** to send it
5. Claude will confirm it is now acting as PromptLens
6. In your **next message**, paste the prompt you want analyzed
7. PromptLens will run the full analysis

✅ Done. Claude is now PromptLens for this conversation.

> ⚠️ **Note:** This only lasts for the current conversation. When you start a new chat,
> you'll need to paste it again — unless you use Method 2 below.

---

### Method 2 — Always On (Claude Pro / Free with User Preferences)
Best for: Using PromptLens every time without re-pasting

1. Go to [claude.ai](https://claude.ai)
2. Click your **profile icon** (bottom left)
3. Click **"Settings"**
4. Click **"User Preferences"** in the left menu
5. In the text box, paste the entire PromptLens text
6. Click **Save**

✅ Done. Every new Claude conversation will now automatically have PromptLens active.
You don't need to paste anything again — just start chatting and paste your prompt.

### Method 2 — Always On (Custom Instructions)
Best for: Using PromptLens in every ChatGPT conversation automatically

1. Go to [chatgpt.com](https://chatgpt.com)
2. Click your **profile icon** (bottom left)
3. Click **"Customize ChatGPT"**
4. You'll see two text boxes:
   - Top box: "What would you like ChatGPT to know about you?"
   - Bottom box: "How would you like ChatGPT to respond?"
5. Paste the entire PromptLens text into the **bottom box**
6. Click **Save**

✅ Done. Every new ChatGPT conversation will now use PromptLens automatically.

> 💡 **Tip:** If you use both boxes, put your personal info in the top box and
> PromptLens in the bottom box. They work together.

---

### Method 3 — Create a Custom GPT (ChatGPT Plus only)
Best for: Sharing PromptLens with others via a link

1. Go to [chatgpt.com](https://chatgpt.com)
2. Click **"Explore GPTs"** in the left sidebar
3. Click **"Create"** (top right)
4. Click **"Configure"** tab
5. In the **"Instructions"** box, paste the entire PromptLens text
6. Give it a name: **PromptLens**
7. Add a description: *"Analyzes and improves your AI prompts"*
8. Click **Save** → Set visibility to **"Anyone with a link"**
9. Share the link with anyone

✅ Done. Anyone who clicks your link gets PromptLens instantly — no copy-pasting needed.

## 📋 How to Use PromptLens Once It's Active

Once you've activated PromptLens using any method above, here's how a session works:

### Step 1 — Paste your prompt or describe your task
You can paste:
- A prompt you've already written
- A vague idea ("I want to write a report about X")
- Something that didn't work well before ("The AI gave me a useless answer when I asked...")

### Step 2 — Read the diagnosis (don't skip this!)
PromptLens will show you a **score table** like this:

| Dimension | Score | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| 🎯 Clarity | 6/10 | Request could mean two different things |
| 📐 Specificity | 3/10 | No format, length, or audience specified |
| 🧠 Context | 2/10 | No background given |
| 🌀 Hallucination Risk | 4/10 | Likely to invent statistics |
| 🪙 Token Efficiency | 5/10 | Some unnecessary words |
| 🧩 Context Window | 4/10 | Wasted context opportunity |
| **Overall** | **4/10** | Needs significant improvement |

Read this carefully — it's the most valuable part.

### Step 3 — Read the explanation
PromptLens will explain in plain English what each weak dimension means for your output.
This is the teaching step — it's what makes you better at prompting over time.

### Step 4 — See how the AI reads your prompt
PromptLens shows you the AI's "internal reading" of your request — what it assumed,
what it guessed, where it took shortcuts. Eye-opening every time.

### Step 5 — Use the improved prompt
PromptLens gives you a rewritten version. You can:
- **Use it as-is** — copy it and send it to any AI
- **Tweak it** — ask PromptLens to adjust the tone, length, or format
- **Learn from it** — compare it to your original and see what changed

### Step 6 — Apply the lesson
PromptLens ends with 3 takeaways. These are the things to remember for next time.

## 💡 Tips for Getting the Most Out of PromptLens

**Tell PromptLens your goal, not just your prompt**
Instead of just pasting a prompt, say:
*"I want to get a professional report I can send to my manager. Here's my prompt: [prompt]"*
This helps PromptLens give more relevant advice.

**Use it iteratively**
After PromptLens improves your prompt, send the improved version to your AI of choice.
If the output still isn't right, bring it back to PromptLens and say:
*"The improved prompt gave me this output — it's still not quite right because [reason].
Can you refine the prompt further?"*

**Use it to learn, not just to get answers**
Read the diagnosis every time, even when your prompt scores well. The goal is to
internalize the principles so you eventually don't need PromptLens at all.

**Try it on prompts that failed you before**
Got a bad AI answer last week? Bring that original prompt to PromptLens and find out
exactly why it failed. It's the fastest way to learn.

## 🔍 What PromptLens Is NOT

- ❌ It won't write content for you — it helps you ask for it better
- ❌ It won't fix the AI's knowledge gaps — it reduces how often the AI guesses
- ❌ It's not a guarantee of perfect output — it dramatically improves your odds
- ❌ It's not just for experts — it's built specifically for everyday users
